Research

My research focuses primarily on value theory, moral psychology, ethics, philosophy of emotion, and well-being. I am especially interested in the epistemic role of emotions and their relationship to moral intuition, rational agency, and practical reasoning.

Current Research Projects

Emotions and Moral Knowledge

This project investigates whether emotions can provide justificatory support for moral beliefs and how emotional experiences contribute to our understanding of morally salient features of the world.

Phenomenological Conservatism and Moral Intuition

I am exploring the relationship between phenomenological conservatism and moral intuition, with particular attention to whether emotional seemings can function as sources of prima facie justification.

Emotion, Reasons, and Rational Agency

This research examines how emotions interact with reasons, agency, and deliberation, and whether emotional responses can reveal normative features of situations that are otherwise difficult to access.
